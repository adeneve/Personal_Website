What am i doing?

Right now I am trying to enter the IT & network engineering career field. After some thought and a few months of retail work, I've decided that network engineering is the most interesting to me. Whats the difference between switches and routers? How do routing protocols work? What is going on in the Internet Service Provider network? Understanding low level computer communication is vital to many existing and widely used applications, and that is why I see so much value in it.

Most routers and switches used in the internet today are Cisco brand. So, it would make sense to understand how to work with their devices if wanting to start a career in network engineering. Cisco offers a bunch of certifications ranging from network security to data center configuration. But, their most universal certifications are the CCNA, CCNP, and CCIE (in order of merit). They recently added the CCENT as a halfway milestone towards aquiring the CCNA due to the large amount of information covered in the CCNA. In Feburary of 2019, I aquired my CCENT, and am currently working to finish my CCNA. My work day consists of studying networking concepts, applying to IT jobs, and helping my dad with some of his construction projects.
